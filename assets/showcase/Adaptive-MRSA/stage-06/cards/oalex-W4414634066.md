# FreeGraftor: Training-Free Cross-Image Feature Grafting for Subject-Driven Text-to-Image Generation

## Cite_Key
yao2025freegraftor

## Problem
Subject-driven image generation faces a trade-off between fidelity and efficiency: tuning-based methods are expensive and slow, while zero-shot methods often fail to preserve subject identity well. Extending this to multi-subject generation further increases the difficulty of preserving identity while staying text aligned.

## Method
FreeGraftor is a training-free framework based on cross-image feature grafting. It performs semantic matching and position-constrained attention fusion to transfer visual details from reference subjects into generated images. It also introduces a noise initialization strategy to preserve geometry priors from reference subjects and facilitate robust feature matching.

The abstract describes extensive qualitative and quantitative experiments on subject-driven and multi-subject text-to-image generation. Specific datasets are not named in the abstract.

## Findings
FreeGraftor significantly outperforms existing zero-shot and training-free approaches in both subject fidelity and text alignment without requiring fine-tuning or extra training. The method also extends naturally to multi-subject generation, improving practicality for deployment.
